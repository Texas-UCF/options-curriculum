Options get their name because the option buyer gains the right or option to trade the underlying. The option seller, however, has the obligation. If the option is used:
  • The buyer exercises his or her right
  • The seller is assigned
If the option is not used:
  • The option expires
  • Neither the buyer nor seller have a position in the option anymore
For example, supposed I sell you an option. You have the right to exercise your option whenever you want, and if it is exercised, I am obligated to fulfill my end of the contract. If you choose not to exercise your right, you can let the option expire. You, the option holder, have all the power.
