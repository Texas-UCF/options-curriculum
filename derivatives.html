Options are a type of derivative. Derivatives are simply contracts or agreements between two parties. The value of derivatives is derived from some underlying product, as opposed to assets like stocks or bonds which represent ownership of something. Examples of derivatives in finance:
  • Options
  • Futures
  • Swaps
  • Mortgage Backed Securities
  • Credit Default Swaps
Examples of derivatives outside of finance:
  • Bet that Donald Trump will be the next President of the United States
  • Agreement to pay a friend $1 for every game the Houston Rockets win this season
 The underlying product for derivatives can be almost anything: stocks, indices, commodities, or even other derivatives.
